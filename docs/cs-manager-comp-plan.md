# CS Manager Compensation Plan

## Role Overview

Player-coach CS Manager responsible for managing her own book of business while leading a team of CSMs. This plan incentivizes excellence in both individual client work and team leadership equally.

## Compensation Structure

| Component | Type | Amount |
|-----------|------|--------|
| Base Salary | Fixed | $250,000 |
| P1: Her Book | Variable (capped) | Up to $50,000 |
| P2: Team's Book | Variable (capped) | Up to $50,000 |
| New Business | Commission (uncapped) | 4% of closed new ACV |

**Target total comp:** $320,000 (base + both pillars at target)
**Stretch total comp:** $350,000+ (base + both pillars at stretch + new business)

---

## P1: Her Book — 50% of Variable ($50,000 max)

Measured on net revenue retention (NRR) across her personally managed accounts.

| Tier | NRR Threshold | Payout |
|------|---------------|--------|
| Below Floor | <95% | $0 |
| Floor | 95% | $25,000 |
| Target | 100% | $35,000 |
| Stretch | 110%+ | $50,000 |

Payout is linear between tiers:

- 95% = $25,000
- 97% = $29,000
- 100% = $35,000
- 105% = $42,500
- 110% = $50,000

---

## P2: Team's Book — 50% of Variable ($50,000 max)

Measured on aggregate NRR across all accounts managed by her CSM team (excludes her own book).

| Tier | NRR Threshold | Payout |
|------|---------------|--------|
| Below Floor | <92% | $0 |
| Floor | 92% | $25,000 |
| Target | 97% | $35,000 |
| Stretch | 105%+ | $50,000 |

Payout is linear between tiers:

- 92% = $25,000
- 94% = $29,000
- 97% = $35,000
- 101% = $42,500
- 105% = $50,000

**Why are P2 thresholds lower than P1?** She influences her team's outcomes through coaching, playbooks, and process — but her CSMs are the ones in the accounts. The 3-5 point gap accounts for the difference between direct execution and indirect leadership.

---

## New Business Commission — Uncapped

4% of annual contract value (ACV) on new logos she closes. Paid as the client pays (not on signing).

| New ACV Closed | Commission |
|----------------|------------|
| $250,000 | $10,000 |
| $500,000 | $20,000 |
| $1,000,000 | $40,000 |
| $2,000,000 | $80,000 |

She must be the closer on the deal. This is not a referral fee — she is responsible for bringing the deal across the finish line.

## Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Rough Year

Everything underperforms. She's ramping, market is tough, team is new.

| Component | Result | Payout |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Base | — | $250,000 |
| P1 | Her book: 93% NRR (below floor) | $0 |
| P2 | Team book: 90% NRR (below floor) | $0 |
| New Business | No new deals closed | $0 |
| **Total comp** | | **$250,000** |

She's still on a $250k base. Not a punishment — but the zero bonus is a serious conversation. Either wrong fit, wrong market, or something structural needs to change.

---

### Scenario 2: Below Target but Not a Disaster

She keeps things from falling apart but doesn't hit her numbers cleanly.

| Component | Result | Payout |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Base | — | $250,000 |
| P1 | Her book: 96% NRR (between floor and target) | ~$27,000 |
| P2 | Team book: 93% NRR (between floor and target) | ~$27,000 |
| New Business | Closed $200k in new ACV | $8,000 |
| **Total comp** | | **~$312,000** |

Below target but still a $300k+ year. The comp reflects that she showed up and did work — just not enough to move the needle significantly.

---

### Scenario 3: Solid Year — Hits Target on Both Sides

This is the expected outcome for a strong performer.

| Component | Result | Payout |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Base | — | $250,000 |
| P1 | Her book: 102% NRR (between target and stretch) | ~$38,000 |
| P2 | Team book: 99% NRR (between target and stretch) | ~$38,500 |
| New Business | Closed $500k in new ACV | $20,000 |
| **Total comp** | | **~$346,500** |

She did her job well on both fronts and brought in some new business. This is what a good year looks like.

---

### Scenario 4: Great Player, Developing Manager

She crushes her own book but is still building her team's capabilities.

| Component | Result | Payout |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Base | — | $250,000 |
| P1 | Her book: 112% NRR (above stretch) | $50,000 |
| P2 | Team book: 95% NRR (between floor and target) | ~$31,000 |
| New Business | Closed $750k in new ACV | $30,000 |
| **Total comp** | | **~$361,000** |

Strong outcome, but she left money on the table on P2. The structure shows her exactly where to invest more energy next year — coaching her team to perform at a higher level.

---

### Scenario 5: Best Case — Elite on All Fronts

She grows her book, her team crushes it, and she's closing new business.

| Component | Result | Payout |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Base | — | $250,000 |
| P1 | Her book: 115% NRR (above stretch) | $50,000 |
| P2 | Team book: 108% NRR (above stretch) | $50,000 |
| New Business | Closed $2M in new ACV | $80,000 |
| **Total comp** | | **$430,000** |

This is the unicorn year. She maxed both pillars and brought in significant new revenue. The uncapped new business commission is what takes her comp from $350k to $430k. She earned every dollar — the company likely retained and grew millions in ARR and added $2M in new business because of her.

---

## Summary

| Scenario | P1 | P2 | New Biz | Total Comp |
|----------|----|----|---------|------------|
| Rough year | $0 | $0 | $0 | $250,000 |
| Below target | ~$27k | ~$27k | $8k | ~$312,000 |
| Solid year | ~$38k | ~$38.5k | $20k | ~$346,500 |
| Great player, developing manager | $50k | ~$31k | $30k | ~$361,000 |
| Elite on all fronts | $50k | $50k | $80k | $430,000 |

The plan is simple: half your bonus comes from your clients, half comes from your team's clients. New business commission is the uncapped upside for those who can do all three.
